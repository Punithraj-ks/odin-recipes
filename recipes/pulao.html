Vegetable Pulao Recipe

Brief history

Pulao, an aromatic rice dish, originated in ancient Persia, known as pilaf. The culinary tradition spread along trade routes and reached the Indian subcontinent during the Mughal era. In India, pulao evolved with diverse regional influences, incorporating local ingredients and spices. Over time, it became a popular and adaptable dish enjoyed globally. Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, corn, etc.)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt (optional)
  • 1/4 cup cashews or almonds (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or cooking oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 4-5 whole peppercorns
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice: Wash the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.

  2. Prep Vegetables: Chop the mixed vegetables into bite-sized pieces. You can use a combination of carrots, peas, beans, corn, or any other vegetables of your choice.

  3. Sauté the whole spices: Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and bay leaf. Sauté until the spices release their aroma.

  4. Add onions and cashews: Add thinly sliced onions and cashews (if using). Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.

  5. Add ginger-garlic paste: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.

  6. Add tomatoes and spices: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well.

  7. Add vegetables: Add the chopped vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.

  8. Drain and add rice: Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pan. Mix gently to coat the rice with the spices.

  9. Add water and salt: Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt according to your taste. Stir well.

  10. Cook the pulao: If using a pressure cooker, close the lid without the weight and cook on medium heat for one whistle. If using a pan, cover it with a tight-fitting lid and simmer on low heat until the rice is cooked and the water is absorbed (about 15-20 minutes).

  11. Fluff the rice: Once the rice is cooked, let it sit for a few minutes. Then, gently fluff the rice with a fork to separate the grains.

  12. Garnish and serve: Garnish the vegetable pulao with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with raita or yogurt.

Enjoy your delicious homemade vegetable pulao!
